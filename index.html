About me

I am a PhD student at the Centre for Complex Systems, University of Sydney. I study collective intelligence and emerging behaviour at criticality. My work explores how and why collective systems self-organise to the critical regimes, and looks at strategies to harness self-organisation for purposeful results. I am broadly interested in statistics, statistical physics, information theory and control theory.

I hold a Master of Engineering (Mechatronics) from the University of Melbourne and a Bachelor of Actuarial Studies from Macquarie University. Before academia, I worked as an actuarial analyst in the reinsurance industry, focusing on risk evaluations of extreme natural hazards such as earthquakes and cyclones.
